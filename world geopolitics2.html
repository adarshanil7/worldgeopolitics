The Heavyweights

In geopolitics, size matters. We measure power primarily through GDP (Economic Output) and Military Spending. The US and China are currently the dominant "Superpowers," creating a bipolar dynamic, though rising powers like India are shifting us toward a "Multipolar" world.

The Board: Choke Points

Geography dictates strategy. "Choke Points" are narrow geographic channels (straits, canals) where global trade is forced to pass. Controlling these points means controlling the flow of oil, food, and goods. High traffic often leads to high geopolitical tension.
