1955 — 1975

The Quagmire

"We are winning, but I don't know who 'we' are anymore."

I. Into the Jungle II. Domino Theory III. Tet Offensive IV. The Fall

KEY FIGURES

  • Ho Chi Minh
  • Lyndon B. Johnson
  • Gen. Westmoreland

It was a war of helicopters against tunnels. The world's greatest military power descended into the jungles of Southeast Asia to stop Communism, only to find itself trapped in a green hell where the enemy was everywhere and nowhere.

II. The Domino Theory

"If you let one country fall to Communism, the rest will follow like a row of dominoes."

— President Eisenhower

This single idea drove American policy for two decades. Vietnam itself was irrelevant to US security, but the *symbolism* of losing it was unacceptable. The US escalated from a few hundred "advisors" to over 500,000 ground troops.

III. The Tet Offensive (1968)

US Generals promised victory was near. Then, on the Vietnamese New Year (Tet), the Viet Cong launched massive coordinated attacks on over 100 cities. Militarily, the US crushed the offensive. But psychologically, they lost the war. The American public realized they had been lied to. The war was nowhere near over.

IV. The Fall of Saigon

In 1973, the US withdrew. In 1975, North Vietnamese tanks burst through the gates of the Presidential Palace in Saigon. The long war was over. Vietnam was united under Communism. The Domino Theory was proven partially wrong; while Laos and Cambodia fell, the rest of Asia did not. The US had lost 58,000 lives for a theory.
