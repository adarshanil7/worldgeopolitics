1944

The Invasion

"The eyes of the world are upon you." — Eisenhower

I. The Fortress II. The Landings III. The Breakout

Hitler had turned Europe into a fortress. The "Atlantic Wall" stretched from Norway to Spain. To defeat the Nazis, the Allies had to breach this wall. It was the largest gamble in military history.

II. The Landings (June 6)

"Omaha Beach was a slaughterhouse."

Thousands of ships and planes delivered 156,000 men to five beaches in Normandy: Utah, Omaha, Gold, Juno, and Sword. On Omaha Beach, Americans faced withering machine-gun fire. But inch by bloody inch, they took the cliffs.

III. The Race to Berlin

Once the beachhead was secured, the Allies poured in men and tanks. They liberated Paris in August. The opening of the Second Front relieved pressure on the Soviets and spelled the beginning of the end for the Third Reich. Less than a year later, Germany surrendered.

SOURCES: "D-Day" - Ambrose
