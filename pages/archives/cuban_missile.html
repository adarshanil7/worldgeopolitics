1962

13 Days

"We went eyeball to eyeball and the other fellow just blinked."

I. The Discovery II. The Brink III. The Secret Deal

It was the closest humanity has ever come to extinction. On October 14, 1962, a U-2 spy plane flying over Cuba took photos proving the impossible: The Soviet Union was building nuclear missile sites just 90 miles from Florida.

II. On the Brink

"The smell of burning hung in the air of the Oval Office."

President Kennedy's generals urged him to bomb the sites immediately. JFK knew that would trigger WWIII. Instead, he ordered a naval "Quarantine" (Blockade) of Cuba to stop Soviet ships. For 13 days, the world held its breath as US Navy destroyers faced down Soviet freighters.

III. The Secret Deal

The crisis didn't end with a battle, but a whisper. JFK's brother, Robert Kennedy, negotiated a secret trade with the Soviet ambassador:

  • The USSR would remove missiles from Cuba publicly.
  • The US would pledge not to invade Cuba.
  • The US would secretly remove its own nuclear missiles from Turkey.
It was a face-saving compromise for Khrushchev that saved the world.
