1945 — PRESENT

The Peace Project

"Whatever the price of the European Union, the price of war is higher."

I. Never Again II. Coal & Steel III. The Superstate

After 1945, Europe was a graveyard. The leaders of France and Germany realized a terrifying truth: if they remained sovereign nations competing for resources, a Third World War was inevitable.

II. Binding the Hands

The solution was genius. They didn't just sign a peace treaty; they physically fused their economies. The European Coal and Steel Community (1951) put the raw materials of war (coal and steel) under a supranational authority. It became physically impossible for France or Germany to build an army against the other without the other knowing.

III. The Modern Experiment

Today, the EU is the world's largest regulatory superpower. It is a Confederation of sovereign states who have voluntarily pooled their sovereignty for peace and profit. It is messy, bureaucratic, and often slow—but it is the only successful alternative to Empire that Europe has ever found.
