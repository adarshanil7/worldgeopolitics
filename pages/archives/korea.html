1950 — PRESENT

The Forgotten War

"Technically, the war never ended."

I. 38th Parallel II. The Invasion III. Enter the Dragon IV. The Standoff

After WWII, Korea was freed from Japan but sliced in half by the Allies. The US took the South; the Soviets took the North. The dividing line was the 38th Parallel. It was supposed to be temporary. It became permanent.

II. The Invasion (1950)

"We will wash our hands in the southern sea." — Kim Il-sung

North Korean forces, backed by Stalin, stormed across the border. They nearly pushed the US/UN forces into the sea. Only a daring amphibious landing at Incheon by Gen. Douglas MacArthur saved the South.

III. Enter the Dragon

As US troops approached the Chinese border, Mao Zedong sent hundreds of thousands of "volunteers" flooding into Korea. The war turned into a bloody stalemate.

IV. The Eternal Standoff

In 1953, an Armistice was signed—not a Peace Treaty. Technically, the war is still on. Today, the Korean Peninsula is the starkest contrast in the world: The South is a futuristic tech giant (Samsung, K-Pop); The North is a hereditary nuclear tyranny in global isolation.
