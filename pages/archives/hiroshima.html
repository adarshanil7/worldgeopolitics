1945

The Sun Rises Twice

"Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds."

I. The Decision II. The Impact III. The Aftermath IV. Nuclear Age

August 6, 1945. It was a clear morning in Hiroshima. At 8:15 AM, the *Enola Gay* dropped "Little Boy". Forty-three seconds later, the city ceased to exist.

II. The Impact

"There were no faces, only eyes."

The fireball was hotter than the surface of the sun. 70,000 people were vaporized instantly. Another 70,000 would die from radiation poisoning in the coming months. Shadows of people were burned into the concrete.

III. Nagasaki

Japan refused to surrender. Three days later, the US dropped "Fat Man" on Nagasaki. Finally, Emperor Hirohito broke with tradition and addressed his people by radio, announcing the surrender. World War II was over.

IV. The Nuclear Age

The Manhattan Project had succeeded, but it opened Pandora's Box. The US monopoly on the bomb lasted only four years until the Soviets tested their own. The Cold War became a hostage crisis where the hostages were the entire human race.
