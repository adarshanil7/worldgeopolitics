1948 — PRESENT

The Holy Land

"Two peoples, one land. A tragedy of right against right."

I. The Roots II. The Wars III. Oslo Failed IV. The Abyss

It is the world's most intractable conflict. Following the Holocaust, Jewish refugees sought safety in their ancestral homeland. The local Arab population (Palestinians) viewed this as colonization.

"The Balfour Declaration (1917) viewed with favor the establishment in Palestine of a national home for the Jewish people."

In 1947, the UN proposed a Partition Plan: 56% for a Jewish State, 43% for an Arab State. Jewish leaders accepted; Arab leaders rejected it as theft. War was inevitable.

II. Total War (1948-1973)

Israel fought three existential wars against its neighbors (Egypt, Syria, Jordan) and won them all.

1948: Israel declares independence. 700,000 Palestinians flee (The Nakba).
1967 (Six-Day War): Israel strikes preemptively, capturing the West Bank, Gaza, and Golan Heights.
1973 (Yom Kippur): A surprise Arab attack almost breaks Israel, but ends in status quo.

III. The Oslo Hope (1993)

For a brief moment, peace seemed possible. Yitzhak Rabin and Yasser Arafat signed the Oslo Accords on the White House lawn. It created the Palestinian Authority and promised a path to statehood.

But extremists on both sides sabotaged it. Rabin was assassinated by a Jewish extremist in 1995. Hamas launched suicide bombings. The "Two-State Solution" began to die.

IV. The Abyss

Today, the conflict is stuck. The West Bank is riddled with settlements, making a Palestinian state geographically impossible. Gaza is ruled by Hamas. The resulting cycle of violence leads only to tragedy, with no end in sight.
