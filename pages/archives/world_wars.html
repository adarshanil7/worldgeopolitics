1914 — 1945

The Machine Age

"The lamps are going out all over Europe, we shall not see them lit again in our life-time."

I. Industrial Death II. The Great War III. The Abyss

The Industrial Revolution promised progress. Instead, it delivered the ability to kill on an industrial scale. When the delicate balance of power in Europe finally snapped in 1914, the result was not a heroic cavalry charge, but a meat grinder.

II. Total War (1914-1918)

It destroyed four empires: The Ottoman, The Austro-Hungarian, The Russian, and The German. It redrew the map of the world, creating the modern Middle East and planting the seeds of bitterness that would lead to round two.

III. The Abyss (1939-1945)

A humiliated Germany turned to the dark charisma of Fascism. The Third Reich sought to build a "Greater Germany" on the bones of Europe. It ended with the Holocaust, the atomic bomb, and the total ruin of the continent. Europe had committed suicide.
