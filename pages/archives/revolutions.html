1789 — 1848

Blood & Ideas

"The Guillotine was the razor that shaved the world clean of Kings."

I. The Guillotine II. Napoleon III. Vienna

It began with bread and ended with blood. The French Revolution (1789) was the watershed moment of modern history. It proved that the "Divine Right of Kings" was a lie. If a King could bleed, he could be replaced.

But unlike the "Bloodless" Glorious Revolution in England (1688), France chose the path of total purification. The Reign of Terror showed the dark side of idealism: that the road to Liberty is often paved with heads.

II. The Napoleon Factor

"I saw the Emperor – this world-soul – riding out of the city on reconnaissance." — Hegel

Napoleon Bonaparte was the Revolution on horseback. He codified its laws (Napoleonic Code) and exported them across Europe at the tip of a bayonet. He destroyed the old Feudal map, dissolving the Holy Roman Empire and planting the seeds of German and Italian nationalism.

III. The Congress of Vienna (1815)

After Napoleon's defeat, the old Monarchs met in Vienna to turn back the clock. Led by Metternich, they tried to suppress Nationalism and Democracy. They succeeded for a few decades (The Concert of Europe), but ideas are bulletproof. By 1848, revolutions were burning across the continent again.
