1955 — 1975

The High Frontier

"We choose to go to the Moon in this decade... not because it is easy, but because it is hard."

I. Soft Power II. Sputnik Shock III. Eagle Has Landed

The Space Race was not about science; it was about survival. It was a contest of ideologies. If Communism could send a man to the stars, perhaps it was the superior system. The winner would capture the imagination of the world.

II. The Sputnik Shock

"Beep... beep... beep..."

In 1957, the USSR launched Sputnik, the first artificial satellite. Americans looked up and panicked. The Soviets had the high ground. Then, in 1961, they launched Yuri Gagarin, the first human in space. America was losing.

III. The Eagle Has Landed

Humiliated, JFK doubled down. He committed the US to landing a man on the Moon. It required the mobilization of 400,000 workers and 4% of the federal budget. But on July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong took that "one small step." The US had won the ultimate race. The USSR never made it to the moon.

SOURCES: "The Right Stuff" - Wolfe
