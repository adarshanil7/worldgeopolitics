2001 — 2021

The Forever War

"September 11, 2001. The day the world changed."

I. Tuesday Morning II. The Response III. The Mistake IV. The Cost

It began with a clear blue sky over New York City. By 10:28 AM, both Twin Towers of the World Trade Center had collapsed. Al-Qaeda, led by Osama Bin Laden, had brought war to American soil. 2,977 people died.

II. The Response

"We will make no distinction between the terrorists who committed these acts and those who harbor them."

— President George W. Bush

The US invaded Afghanistan (Operation Enduring Freedom) to destroy Al-Qaeda and remove the Taliban. It was popular, swift, and initially successful. But it was just the beginning.

III. The Mistake (Iraq)

In 2003, the US invaded Iraq, claiming Saddam Hussein had "Weapons of Mass Destruction" (WMD). No WMDs were ever found. The invasion destabilized the entire Middle East, leading to the rise of ISIS and chaos that continues to this day.

IV. The Cost

The War on Terror cost an estimated $8 Trillion and over 900,000 lives. It fundamentally changed civil liberties (The Patriot Act) and militarized police forces. In 2021, the US withdrew from Afghanistan, and the Taliban returned to power.
