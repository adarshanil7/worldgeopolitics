POWER STATS

The Paranoia of Geography

Russia is the largest country on Earth, but it feels small and vulnerable. It has no natural mountain barriers in the West (The North European Plain), making it easy to invade (Napoleon, Hitler).

1. Warm Water Ports

Most of Russia's ports freeze in winter. It lacks easy access to global trade. This is why Crimea (Black Sea) is existential‚Äîit is their only reliable warm water naval base.

2. Strategic Depth

To feel safe, Russia pushes its borders out as far as possible to create "buffer zones." An independent, NATO-aligned Ukraine destroys this buffer, which Russia views as a direct threat.

The Chronicles: Collapse & Resurgence

1991: The Collapse

End of Empire

The USSR dissolves. Russia loses 1/3 of its territory and 1/2 of its population overnight. The 90s are a decade of humiliation and economic ruin.

2000: Putin Era Begins

State Consolidation

Vladimir Putin takes power. He crushes oligarchs, recentralizes power, and leverages high oil prices to rebuild the military.

2014: Annexation of Crimea

The Pivot

Response to the Maidan Revolution in Ukraine. Russia takes Crimea to secure the Black Sea Fleet. The West imposes sanctions; Russia begins "Pivot to Asia."

2022: Invasion of Ukraine

Total War

Russia launches a full-scale invasion. The goal: dismantle the Ukrainian state. The result: Russia is cut off from the West and becomes dependent on China.

Commodity Superpower

Russia is the #1 or #2 exporter of Wheat, Fertilizer, Gas, and Aluminum. It can hold the global food and energy supply hostage.

War Economy

Currently spending ~6-7% of GDP on defense. While sustainable in the short term, it hollows out civilian infrastructure and education.

The Arctic

Global warming is opening the "Northern Sea Route." Control of this route gives Russia a new maritime corridor to China, bypassing Western chokepoints.
