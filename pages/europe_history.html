1400 - 1700

RENAISSANCE & CAPITALISM

The End of the Dark Ages

The Shift

Europe transitioned from Feudalism (Land nodes owed to Kings) to Capitalism (Trade and Money). The Black Death had killed so many peasants that labor became valuable, breaking serfdom.

Simultaneously, the Renaissance (Rebirth) in Italy brought back Greek/Roman logic, art, and science. The Printing Press (Gutenberg, 1440) democratized knowledge, fueling the Reformation.

Crucially, the Peace of Westphalia (1648) ended the 30 Years' War and established the concept of the Nation State‚Äîsovereign borders that no Pope or Emperor could violate. This is the foundation of modern geopolitics.

Fall of Monarchies

"Liberty, Equality, Fraternity." The French Revolution (1789) proved that Kings were not divine. It was a Bloody Revolution (The Guillotine), contrasting with later "Bloodless Revolutions" or gradual Reformism. It exported the idea of Nationalism across Europe. By 1848, revolutions swept the continent, demanding reforms.

1860 - 1871

RISE OF NATION STATES

Prussia, Italy, and the Smaller Reich

Realpolitik

Italy united under Garibaldi. But the main event was German Unification. Otto von Bismarck united the loose German confederacies into a powerhouse through "Blood and Iron" (Wars with Denmark, Austria, France).

This created the German Empire (The "Smaller German Reich" or Kleindeutschland solution, excluding Austria) in 1871. A massive power in the center of Europe that upset the balance of power, leading directly to the World Wars.

Industrial Death

The Industrial Revolution gave nations mass production. When War came in 1914, it wasn't heroic cavalry charges; it was industrial slaughter (Machine guns, Gas).

Third Reich: After WWI, a humiliated Germany turned to Fascism. Hitler's Third Reich sought to dominate Europe but ended in total ruin in 1945.

INDUSTRY & WAR

The World on Fire

Rise & Fall of Confederacies

Europe has tried many times to unite:
1. Holy Roman Empire: Loose, feudal, ineffective.
2. Napoleonic Empire: Forced union by conquest. Failed.
3. German Confederation: Prelude to the Reich.
4. European Union (Today): The first voluntary, democratic union suitable for the modern age.

Status Today:

The EU stands as the successor to centuries of bloodshed. A confederacy of sovereign states pooling resources to survive in a world of giants.
