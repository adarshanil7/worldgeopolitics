1947 - 1991 GLOBAL

THE COLD WAR

The Iron Curtain

Why It Happened

Following WWII, the world split into two ideological camps: the Capitalist West (USA) and the Communist East (USSR). Without a direct "hot" war due to Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD), the conflict played out through proxy wars, espionage, and a nuclear arms race.

Outcome: USSR Collapse

The Soviet Union dissolved in 1991 due to economic stagnation, internal dissent, and being outspent by the US. However, a "New Cold War" is emerging today.

Peak Nuclear Inventory (Warheads)

The 13 Days

In October 1962, the US discovered Soviet nuclear missiles in Cuba, just 90 miles from Florida. President Kennedy ordered a naval blockade. This was the closest the world ever came to all-out nuclear war.

1955 - 1975 SOUTHEAST ASIA

THE QUAGMIRE

The Vietnam War

Why It Happened

Driven by the "Domino Theory"—the fear that if one country fell to Communism, others would follow. The US intervened to support South Vietnam against the Communist North (Viet Cong).

Status: US Withdrawal

Despite superior firepower, the US could not defeat the guerrilla tactics in the jungle. The US withdrew in 1973, and Saigon fell in 1975, unifying Vietnam under Communism.

Peak Troop Levels (1968)

The Event that Changed Everything

On September 11, 2001, Al-Qaeda terrorists hijacked planes and struck the World Trade Center and Pentagon. This triggered the "War on Terror," leading to the US invasions of Afghanistan (2001) and Iraq (2003).

THE SPACE RACE

USA vs USSR

Why It Happened

It wasn't just about science; it was about showing who had the better system. The Soviets launched the first satellite (Sputnik) and first man (Gagarin). The US responded by aiming for the Moon.

Outcome: US Victory

In 1969, Apollo 11 landed not just Neil Armstrong, but the American Flag on the Moon. This proved US technological dominance and ended the race.

CHERNOBYL

The Invisible Enemy

What Happened?

A safety test gone wrong caused Reactor 4 to explode, releasing 400x more radiation than the Hiroshima bomb. It forced the evacuation of 350,000 people and created a "Ghost City" (Pripyat).

The Impact

The disaster exposed the flaws and secrecy of the Soviet Union, accelerating its collapse. The "Exclusion Zone" remains uninhabitable for humans for 20,000 years.
