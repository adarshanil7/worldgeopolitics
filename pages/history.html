THE COLD WAR

The Iron Curtain

Why It Happened

Following WWII, the world split into two ideological camps: the Capitalist West (USA) and the Communist East (USSR). Without a direct "hot" war due to Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD), the conflict played out through proxy wars, espionage, and a nuclear arms race.

Current Status: New Cold War?

Officially ended with the Soviet collapse in 1991. However, growing tensions between the US and China/Russia suggest a "Cold War 2.0" is beginning, centered on technology and trade rather than ideology.

Why It Happened

Liberated from Japan in 1945, the peninsula was divided by the US and USSR along the 38th Parallel. The North invaded the South in 1950, sparking a brutal 3-year war involving China and the UN.

Current Status: Frozen Conflict

Technically still at war (only an Armistice signed in 1953). The DMZ remains the world's most militarized border. The South became a tech giant, while the North became a nuclear pariah.

THE PARTITION

The Largest Migration in History

Why It Happened

As the British Raj exited, religious tensions led to the demand for a separate Muslim homeland (Pakistan). The hasty drawing of the Radcliffe Line displaced 15 million people and killed 1-2 million in sectarian violence.

Current Status: Nuclear Standoff

Created the Kashmir dispute, leading to four wars. Both nations are now nuclear-armed, making the border one of the most dangerous flashpoints on Earth.

Competing national movements (Zionism vs Arab Nationalism) for the same territory between the Jordan River and the Mediterranean. Exacerbated by British colonial promises and the horrors of the Holocaust leading to the 1948 forming of Israel.
