THE COLD WAR

The Iron Curtain

Why It Happened

Containment Theory: Architect George Kennan argued that the USSR was inherently expansionist and must be "contained" until it collapsed from within.

After WWII, the "Iron Curtain" descended, splitting the world between the Capitalist West (NATO) and the Communist East (Warsaw Pact). Without a direct "hot" war due to Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD), the conflict played out through bloody proxy wars (Korea, Vietnam), ruthless espionage (CIA vs KGB), and a race for technological supremacy.

Outcome: USSR Collapse

The Soviet Union dissolved in 1991 due to economic stagnation, internal dissent, and being outspent by the US. However, a "New Cold War" is emerging today.

The 13 Days

In response to US "Jupiter" missiles in Turkey, Premier Khrushchev secretly deployed nuclear missiles to Cuba. When U-2 spy planes discovered them, the JFK administration faced a choice: Airstrike (risking WWIII) or Blockade.

Kennedy chose a naval "Quarantine". After tense back-channel negotiations, a secret deal was struck: Russia withdrew from Cuba, and the US quietly removed missiles from Turkey. It remains the closest humanity has ever come to extinction.

THE QUAGMIRE

The Vietnam War

Why It Happened

Driven by the "Domino Theory"‚Äîthe fear that if one country fell to Communism, its neighbors would topple like dominoes. The US intervened to support South Vietnam against the Communist North (Ho Chi Minh) to stop the "Red Spread" in Southeast Asia.

Status: US Withdrawal

Despite overwhelming firepower (napalm, helicopter cavalry), the US could not defeat the guerrilla tactics of the Viet Cong in the jungle. The Tet Offensive (1968) was a military failure for the North but a psychological victory, proving the war was nowhere near over.

The US withdrew in 1973 (Paris Peace Accords), and Saigon fell in 1975, unifying Vietnam under Communism. It proved that willpower could defeat superior technology.

THE KOREAN SPLIT

The 38th Parallel

Status: Frozen Conflict

After WWII, the US and USSR arbitrarily divided Korea at the 38th Parallel. In 1950, the North (Kim Il-sung) invaded the South. It became the first "proxy war".

China's massive intervention saved the North from defeat. The 1953 Armistice halted fighting, but no peace treaty was arguably ever signed. Today, it is a standoff between a hyper-capitalist tech giant and a hereditary nuclear tyranny.

Why It Happened

As the British Raj exited, religious tensions boiled over. Lord Mountbatten advanced the date of independence, and the hastily drawn Radcliffe Line carved the subcontinent into India and Pakistan.

This reckless division engaged 15 million people in the largest mass migration in history, sparking communal riots that killed up to 2 million. The Princely State of Kashmir's disputed accession remains a nuclear flashpoint today.

THE HOLY LAND

Israel & Palestine

Status: Active War

Rooted in the 1947 UN Partition Plan which was rejected by Arab leaders. Recurring wars (1948, 1967 Six-Day War, 1973 Yom Kippur) defined the borders.

The Oslo Accords (1993) failed to deliver a two-state solution. Issues like the "Right of Return," settlements, and the status of Jerusalem remain unsolved, leading to the tragedies of the Intifadas and the 2023 Gaza War.

THE SPACE RACE

USA vs USSR

Why It Happened

A battle for absolute "Soft Power". The Soviets shocked the West with Sputnik (1957) and Yuri Gagarin (1961), proving Communism could produce superior science. The US needed a victory to prove Capitalist supremacy.

Outcome: US Victory

In 1969, Apollo 11 landed not just Neil Armstrong, but the American Flag on the Moon. This proved US technological dominance and ended the race.

CHERNOBYL

The Invisible Enemy

What Happened?

Due to a critical design flaw in the RBMK reactors and human error during a safety test, Reactor 4 exploded. The Soviet Union attempted to cover it up, but radiation clouds drifted over Sweden, forcing them to admit the catastrophe.

The Impact

The disaster exposed the flaws and secrecy of the Soviet Union, accelerating its collapse. The "Exclusion Zone" remains uninhabitable for humans for 20,000 years.

The Day the Sun Rose Twice

August 1945. To force Japan's surrender without a bloody land invasion, the US dropped two atomic bombs. "Little Boy" on Hiroshima and "Fat Man" on Nagasaki.

Outcome: The Nuclear Age Begins

Japan surrendered, ending WWII. But the Manhattan Project's success changed the world forever. Humanity now possessed the power to destroy itself. This sparked the Cold War arms race, as the monopoly on atomic weapons was short-lived.
