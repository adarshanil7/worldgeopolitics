POWER STATS

Geography: The Peninsula

Europe is a "peninsula of peninsulas" on the western edge of Asia. Its coastline invites trade, but its fractured geography (Alps, Pyrenees, Danube) created diverse, competing nation-states that warred for centuries.

1. Energy Poverty

Europe has little domestic oil/gas. It built its modern industry on cheap Russian gas. The loss of this supply is the central crisis of the 2020s.

2. The Northern Plain

The flat land from France to Russia allows for easy movement of armies. This is why NATO exists‚Äîto prevent any single power (Russia or Germany) from dominating this plain again.

The Chronicles: From War to Union

1957: Treaty of Rome

Never Again

After destroying itself in WWII, Europe decides to bind its economies (Coal & Steel) so tightly that war becomes impossible. The EEC is born.

1999: The Euro

Monetary Union

A bold experiment to create a rival to the Dollar. It creates huge efficiency but leaves Southern Europe without the ability to devalue currency during crises.

2016: Brexit

The Splinter

The UK votes to leave. It shatters the myth of "Ever Closer Union." The EU responds by becoming more cohesive and tough in negotiations.

2022: Zeitenwende

Strategic Awakening

Russia's invasion wakes Europe from its pacifist slumber. Germany re-arms. The EU bans Russian oil. Geopolitics returns to the continent.

The Brussels Effect

Because the EU Single Market is so rich, companies globally follow EU rules (GDPR, USB-C). Note: The EU exports regulations, not tech.

Green Deal

The EU is betting its future on Green Tech to solving energy dependency. The CBAM (Carbon Tax) is a tool to force other nations to decarbonize.

Demographics

Europe is dying. Italy, Germany, and Spain have shrinking populations. Without immigration (which is politically toxic), the welfare state will collapse.
